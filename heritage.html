Cultural Heritage

Monuments, milestones, festivals and traditions that shape the story of India.

Living history

Heritage Monuments

UNESCO-recognised and nationally protected sites that define India's architectural legacy.

Taj Mahal

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, completed in 1653.

Hill Forts of Rajasthan

Six majestic forts, including Amber and Mehrangarh, inscribed together as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Konark Sun Temple

A 13th-century temple shaped like a colossal chariot with intricately carved stone wheels and horses.

Through the ages

Historical Timeline

c. 2500 BCE

The Indus Valley Civilisation flourishes with planned cities like Mohenjo-daro and Harappa.

c. 320 CE

The Gupta Empire ushers in a golden age of art, science and literature.

1526

The Mughal Empire is founded, later producing landmarks such as the Taj Mahal and Red Fort.

1947

India gains independence, beginning a new chapter of nation-building and cultural revival.

Today

Thousands of heritage sites, festivals and living traditions continue to be preserved and celebrated.

Celebration

Traditional Festivals

Diwali

The festival of lights, marked with oil lamps, fireworks and sweets celebrating good over evil.

Holi

The festival of colours, welcoming spring with powder colours, music and dance in the streets.

Onam

Kerala's harvest festival, famous for snake-boat races, flower carpets (pookalam) and grand feasts.

Movement & music

Famous Classical Dances

Bharatanatyam

A classical dance form from Tamil Nadu known for its precise footwork and expressive storytelling.

Kathak

A North Indian dance form characterised by fast spins and rhythmic footwork, rooted in storytelling.

Kathakali

A Kerala dance-drama recognised by elaborate makeup, costumes and dramatic facial expressions.

Taste of tradition

Traditional Food

Regional Thalis

A complete meal served on one platter, varying by region but always balancing flavour and nutrition.

Biryani

A fragrant layered rice dish with roots in Mughal royal kitchens, now beloved across the country.

Mithai (Sweets)

Milk- and sugar-based sweets like ladoo and jalebi, central to every festival and celebration.

Made by hand

Handicrafts & Local Traditions

Handloom Weaving

Generations-old weaving traditions producing textiles like Banarasi silk and Pochampally ikat.

Blue Pottery & Ceramics

Jaipur's distinctive blue pottery, made without clay, using quartz stone powder and glaze.

Rangoli

Colourful floor art made from rice powder or flower petals, welcoming guests and good fortune.
